Butter Chicken

Description

Butter chicken, a jewel in the crown of Indian cuisine, is a dish that ignites the senses with its aromatic allure and rich, velvety flavor. Succulent pieces of tender chicken, marinated in a symphony of spices and yogurt, are lovingly roasted until they acquire a smoky, charred elegance. This tantalizing chicken then takes a luxurious bath in a velvety tomato and butter-infused sauce, creating a harmonious marriage of flavors. The result is a dish that delights the palate with a tantalizing balance of warmth, creaminess, and a subtle kiss of spice, ensuring every bite is a revelation of culinary craftsmanship.

As you savor each bite of butter chicken, the sensation is an unforgettable journey through the heart of India, where the layers of masala and the richness of the sauce intertwine in a flavorful dance. Paired with fragrant basmati rice or freshly baked naan, this dish transcends the ordinary, leaving an indelible mark of comfort and sophistication on the taste buds, making it a timeless favorite that beckons you back for more.

Ingredients

Main

  • 500 grams boneless chicken (breasts or thighs, breasts may need to be marinated for at least 12 hours)
  • 3/4th to 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4th to 1/3rd teaspoon salt
  • 1/2nd to 3/4th teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (or paprika adjust for taste, reduce for kids)
  • 3/4th to 1 teaspoon garam masala (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (optional)
  • 1/8th teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2nd teaspoon cumin powder (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder (optional)
  • 3/4th to 1 tablespoon oil
  • 3/4th tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1/3rd cup Greek yoghurt or hung curd

Sauce/gravy

  • 2 tablespoon butter
  • 1 cinnamon
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 3/4th tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 to 2 green chilies (deseed, slit or chop, avoid for kids)
  • 1 to 2 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder or paprika (adjust for taste and color)
  • 1 to 1 and 1/2nd teaspoon garam masala (adjust to taste, divided)
  • 1/2nd teaspoon cumin powder (optional)
  • 1 to 1 and 1/2nd teaspoon coriander powder (optional)
  • 1/2nd tablespoon kasuri methi crushed

Steps

Marinate the chicken

  1. Make sure there is no excess moisture on chicken. Pat dry with kitchen tissues/paper if needed. Add red chili powder, salt and lemon juice. Mix will, cover and rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Add ginger garlic paste, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, kasuri methi, turmeric and hung curd/Greek yogurt. Mix well and cover. Let the chicken rest in the marinade for a minimum of 12 hours in the refrigerator. The longer you leave, the more tender and juicer the chicken is going to be. You can leave this for a maximum of 48 hours.
  3. When you are ready to make the sauce, prepare the ingredients. Keep the chicken out of the refrigerator, as we don’t want to grill/ pan-fry the cold chicken.

Prepare the sauce

  1. Blend 600 grams diced tomatoes.
  2. Blend 20 to 22 cashew nuts, add water, blend again.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons butter in a pan. Add cinnamon, cloves and cardamoms. Once they begin to sizzle, add ginger garlic paste, green chilies (leave out for kids) and fry on a medium heat for 1 to 2 minutes till the raw smell goes away. Do not burn.
  4. Turn off the heat. Stir in the red chilli powder, garam masala, cumin powder and coriander powder.
  5. Strain the tomato puree to the pan. Use a sturdy spoon to strain and discard the residue.
  6. Mix well and bring this to a boil on a medium high heat. Be careful as this can easily splatter.
  7. Cover partially and let boil on a medium heat, stirring often, until thick. This takes about 15 mins, depending on the kind of cookware and stove.
  8. Pour 1 and 1/2nd cups hot water and mix well.
  9. Simmer, partially covered for 10 mins or until the sauce thickens & traces of fats/butter are visible on top of the sauce.

Prepare the chicken

Chicken can be prepared in the oven or grilled on the stove top. In this recipe the chicken is going to be pan-fried.

  1. Add one tablespoon oil/butter or ghee and place the chicken pieces spacing them apart. Fry on a medium heat for 2 mins. Ignore the moisture oozing out of the chicken. Turn the chicken pieces to the other side, don’t burn and do not overcook.
  2. Cook until the chicken is just done and all the moisture evaporates.

Make Chicken Makhani

  1. Add chicken to the prepared butter chicken gravy.
  2. Pour 1/2nd cup more hot water if the sauce is too thick and simmer for about 5 to 7 minutes till it becomes tender. This is the time the sauce actually develops the flavors. The cook time may vary here. This additional half cup water is optional depending on how long you cook.
  3. Add 1/2nd to 3/4th teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon sugar, and 1/2nd tablespoon kasuri methi. Stir well and taste test. If needed, add more garam masala and another tablespoon butter for additional flavor.
  4. Stir in 1/4th to 1/3rd cup heavy cream. Or take a few tablespoons of the hot gravy to a small bowl and stir in the cream. Mix and then pour the cream to the butter chicken. This prevents cream from splitting.
