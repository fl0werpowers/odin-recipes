Scrambled eggs

Description

Scrambled eggs, the humble yet tantalizing creation of the breakfast world, are a symphony of simplicity and savoriness. Whisked to a velvety perfection, these golden ribbons of sunshine come to life in a sizzling pan, where they pirouette in buttery splendor. With each gentle stir, they transform into delicate, curd-like clouds of deliciousness, their custardy texture and creamy richness a delight to behold. Seasoned with a pinch of salt, a dash of pepper, and perhaps a hint of chives or a sprinkle of cheese, they dance on the palate, a harmonious melody of comfort and warmth, embodying breakfast bliss.

Ingredients

  • 2 large free range eggs
  • 6 tablespoons single cream or full cream milk
  • a knob of butter

Steps

  1. Lightly whisk 2 large eggs, 6 tbsp single cream or full cream milk and a pinch of salt together until the mixture has just one consistency.
  2. Heat a small non-stick frying pan for a minute or so, then add a knob of butter and let it melt. Donâ€™t allow the butter to brown or it will discolor the eggs.
  3. Pour in the egg mixture and let it sit, without stirring, for 20 seconds. Stir with a wooden spoon, lifting and folding it over from the bottom of the pan.
  4. Let it sit for another 10 seconds then stir and fold again.
  5. Repeat until the eggs are softly set and slightly runny in places. Remove from the heat and leave for a moment to finish cooking.
  6. Give a final stir and serve the velvety scramble without delay.
